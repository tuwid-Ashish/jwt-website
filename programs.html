Welfare Programs & Support Services

From financial assistance in times of crisis to professional development and community events — everything the trust does is for the journalist community.

Financial Assistance

The trust helps journalists and their families access financial support during extreme hardship. This includes direct assistance distributed by the United Press Club as well as guidance to access the Government of India's Journalist Welfare Scheme (JWS).

Government JWS Key Benefits

Situation Aid Available
Death of journalist causing extreme hardship to family Up to ₹5,00,000
Permanent disability preventing livelihood Up to ₹5,00,000
Diagnosis with major listed ailment Up to ₹5,00,000
Serious injury requiring hospitalization Up to ₹5,00,000
Non-accredited journalist (5+ years service) Up to ₹1,00,000 base

Source: Government of India, Ministry of Information and Broadcasting, JWS Guidelines 2019

Eligibility Summary

  • Indian citizen and resident
  • Accredited to PIB at Central or State/UT Government level, OR
  • Working continuously as a journalist for a minimum of 5 years (non-accredited)

Health Support

Journalists accredited with the Government of India are entitled to treatment at Government and CGHS empanelled private hospitals at CGHS rates. The trust helps journalists and their families understand their entitlements and access the healthcare facilities available to them under government welfare programs.

Government Hospital Access

Accredited journalists can access government hospitals for free or subsidized treatment under national health programs.

CGHS Empanelled Private Hospitals

Access to private hospitals at CGHS rates through government-approved empanelment schemes for accredited journalists.

Health Scheme Guidance

Expert guidance to navigate complex health entitlements and maximize benefits available under government schemes.

Emergency Medical Support

Assistance and coordination for emergency medical situations requiring immediate hospitalization and financial support.

Training & Professional Development

The trust facilitates training programs for journalists at all career stages. Areas covered include digital journalism tools, fact-checking practices, legal awareness for journalists, safety training, and career mentorship. Programs are organized in partnership with press clubs, media institutions, and professional associations.

💻

Digital Journalism

Master modern tools and platforms for multimedia reporting, social media engagement, and digital storytelling.

🔍

Fact-Checking

Learn verified techniques for source verification, misinformation detection, and ethical reporting practices.

⚖️

Legal Awareness

Understand press freedom laws, defamation protection, right to information, and journalist rights in India.

🛡️

Safety Training

Training for journalists covering dangerous situations, conflict zones, and high-risk reporting scenarios.

🎯

Career Mentorship

One-on-one guidance from experienced journalists for career advancement and professional growth.

📊

Data Journalism

Learn data analysis, visualization, and storytelling techniques for impactful investigative reporting.

Community & Tribute Events

The trust organizes community events including tribute functions to honor journalists who have passed away. At these events, financial assistance cheques are distributed to the families of departed journalists. The United Press Club (Regd.) organized one such function at Guru Nanak Dev Bhawan, Ludhiana, where Rs.51,000 cheques were presented to each beneficiary family.

Tribute Functions

Annual ceremonies honoring the memory of departed journalists and celebrating their contributions to journalism and society.

Financial Assistance Distribution

Direct assistance payments to families of departed journalists during tribute ceremonies and community gatherings.

Community Meetups

Regular gatherings for journalists to network, share experiences, and build professional relationships across regions.

Journalist Premier League

Annual sports tournament celebrating the journalism community and honoring the courage of journalists in their profession.
